Research Domain

Domain & Methodology

An in-depth look at our research foundation, identified gaps, objectives, and the technical approach behind UrinoAI.

📖 Literature Survey

Extensive review of existing work in automated urinalysis and medical image classification revealed several key insights:

  • Traditional urine sediment analysis relies on manual microscopy by trained pathologists, which is subjective and time-intensive (Fogazzi et al., 2020).
  • Recent studies have applied CNNs such as ResNet, VGG, and Inception for medical image classification with promising results in pathology (Litjens et al., 2017).
  • YOLO-based object detection has been explored for cell detection in blood smear images, demonstrating real-time capability (Alam & Islam, 2019).
  • Transfer learning from ImageNet pre-trained models significantly improves performance on small medical imaging datasets (Raghu et al., 2019).
  • Existing commercial systems (e.g., Sysmex UF-5000) are expensive and inaccessible for low-resource healthcare facilities.

🔍 Research Gap

Despite advances in AI-based medical imaging, significant gaps remain in urine microscopy analysis:

  • Most existing solutions focus on a limited set of particle types (typically RBC and WBC only).
  • Lack of publicly available, well-annotated urine sediment datasets for comprehensive model training.
  • Few systems address the full pipeline from image capture to diagnostic report generation.
  • Existing commercial solutions are prohibitively expensive for developing-country hospitals and clinics.
  • Limited research on real-time detection and quantification of all clinically relevant sediment particles in a single system.

❓ Research Problem

How can deep learning and computer vision be effectively applied to automate the detection, classification, and quantification of diverse urine sediment particles from microscopy images, achieving laboratory-grade accuracy while remaining cost-effective and accessible?

Research Objectives

Project Objectives

Our research aims to achieve the following specific outcomes.

1️⃣

Multi-Class Particle Classification

Develop a CNN-based classifier capable of identifying 12+ urine sediment particle categories including RBC, WBC, epithelial cells, casts, crystals, and bacteria.

2️⃣

Real-Time Object Detection

Implement a YOLO-based detection pipeline to localize and identify individual particles within full microscopy slide images in under 3 seconds.

3️⃣

Automated Quantification

Build an automated cell counting and concentration estimation module that produces clinically meaningful particle counts per high-power field (HPF).

4️⃣

Accessible Web Interface

Design a user-friendly web-based application enabling healthcare workers to upload microscopy images and receive structured diagnostic reports.

⚙️ Methodology

Our research follows a systematic, iterative approach combining data engineering, model development, and system integration:

  • Data Collection: Curating urine sediment microscopy images from hospital laboratories and public datasets, with expert annotation.
  • Data Preprocessing: Applying image augmentation (rotation, flipping, brightness/contrast adjustment), noise reduction, and normalization.
  • Model Architecture: Evaluating multiple CNN architectures (ResNet-50, EfficientNet, MobileNet) for classification; YOLOv8 for detection.
  • Transfer Learning: Fine-tuning pre-trained ImageNet weights on our domain-specific dataset to overcome limited sample sizes.
  • Training & Validation: K-fold cross-validation with stratified splits to ensure robust performance across all particle classes.
  • Evaluation: Using accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, and mAP as primary performance metrics.
  • Integration: Deploying the model behind a Flask/FastAPI backend with a responsive web frontend for end-to-end analysis.
